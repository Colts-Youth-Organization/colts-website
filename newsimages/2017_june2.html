Last week, Colts parent Craig Nowack captured a unique perspective of of the drum corps activity - the line at the cook truck! The goal of the Colts cook truck is to feed 200 people in 20 minutes or less four times a day, and it can be a big undertaking. Some tips from the more experienced Colts cooks include always pre stage 12 - 15 plates, count your plates so you know how many people are left to feed, and make sure there's always enough for left overs! In just a few days, summer tour will be fully underway! The Colts will be taking a new route this summer, performing along the East Coast from Allentown to Boston to Orlando before returning for Music On The March on Friday, July 14. The Colt Cadets will continue to tour around the Midwest, competing in a total of 17 shows throughout the summer.



Volunteers are a vital part of the drum corps experience and allow groups to move down the road in an efficient, well-fed, and organized manner. Both Colts and Colt Cadets are looking for volunteers for this summer! Jobs include cooking, sewing, and van driving. No special skills or previous experience required! Anyone is able to volunteer - parent, alumni, fan, or friend. We also have opportunities for bus and truck driving, which do require a CDL.
